Invisible Narva is a mobile project developed in MEDIT Creative Lab. at Tallinn University. This project has an extensive background. A lot of academics, students, animators, 2D, and 3D artists were involved in the project. I worked alone as a Unity developer. The prototype was completed with a part-time study of approximately four months. Although the final product will be designed as real world-scale AR, touch joysticks are added to reach more people for the prototype.


Beyond the Canopy is a 2D platform game developed by two programmers, two artists, and an animator for the game development course at Tallinn University in less than a month. My role was programming the physics of loose tiles and stones as well as player’s movement.


New World is a school project developed at Tallinn University in almost a week with a programmer, two artists, and a writer. I was responsible for programming. The role of players is to escape from a prison-like room by solving a logic gate puzzle.


How Animals See was an application developed for science centers. Visitors can see the world through the eyes of animals such as cats, dogs, bees, fish, eagles, and horses, thanks to the Oculus placed in six different animal modal figures. Although the background of the project was quite extensive; nevertheless, two employees, one Unity3D developer, and a 3D designer were responsible for developing the digital application.


Blood Vein Travel is a VR game developed for exhibition purposes in science centers. Besides, it was one of my first industrial projects. I developed the game in a month with a 3D designer in a company in Istanbul. There is not any player controller. Players are wearing the Oculus Kit to wander around the blood veins, lungs, heart, and liver. Moreover, they can catch the white and red blood cells by using the gaze of VR google to learn what they are and why they are essential for the human body.


In the CET department of Boğaziçi University, each student has to design and develop an educational game individually. These games generally aim to teach a subject having learning difficulties and misconceptions. After the development, each student should test their games with students in a real lab environment. Tree-house was my undergraduate project developed in more than a year using the ADDIE instructional model.

Walking CET was a project I developed regarding an elective game development course in undergraduate. This game helped me understand sequential tile and game object instantiation. It was my first 2D platformer game that I started to understand how game physics works.


Jigsaw puzzle is a fully functional undergraduate project I developed as part of a content development course with Unity3D. I think this game is a milestone in understanding the game engine and how it works.
